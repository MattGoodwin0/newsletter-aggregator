The Verge February 24, 2026

OpenAI Releases GPT-5 With Dramatic Improvements in Reasoning and Multimodal Tasks

By Sarah Chen & Marcus Webb

OpenAI has officially unveiled GPT-5, its most capable language model to date, claiming significant breakthroughs in multi-step reasoning, code generation, and real-time multimodal interaction. The announcement was made during a livestreamed event from San Francisco, drawing hundreds of thousands of simultaneous viewers.

Independent benchmarks corroborate the company's claims, placing GPT-5 ahead of competing systems on a broad suite of professional tasks including legal analysis, scientific literature review, and software architecture design. Researchers at Stanford noted particular improvements in the model's handling of long-context tasks, sustaining coherence over documents exceeding 200,000 tokens.

The release arrives amid intensifying competition from Google DeepMind, Anthropic, and a growing cluster of open-source efforts. Analysts expect the launch to trigger a new round of capability announcements from rivals within the coming weeks.

Europe's New AI Liability Directive Puts Burden of Proof on Developers

By Elisa Fontaine

The European Parliament has passed a sweeping AI liability directive that fundamentally shifts how responsibility is assigned when automated systems cause harm. Under the new rules, manufacturers and deployers of high-risk AI must demonstrate their systems did not cause damage, rather than leaving that burden to injured parties.

Legal experts describe the legislation as the most consequential piece of AI governance since GDPR, warning that compliance costs could reshape product development timelines for companies operating in the EU market. The directive applies to a broad range of sectors including healthcare, financial services, hiring platforms, and critical infrastructure.

Industry groups have pledged to challenge several provisions before the directive enters force in late 2027, arguing that some disclosure requirements conflict with trade secret protections. The Commission has indicated it will publish detailed implementation guidance by mid-year.

The vote comes just one week after a high-profile incident in which an autonomous logistics system was linked to a warehouse accident in the Netherlands, injecting fresh urgency into the legislative timeline.
